# Naive Prompt vs Structured Command Prompt

This example demonstrates how **structured command prompting**
produces more reliable and usable outputs than a naive prompt.

---

## Naive Prompt


### Issues
- No defined audience
- No scope or depth control
- No output structure
- High variance in responses

---

## Structured Command Prompt

ROLE:
You are an AI Prompt Engineer.

OBJECTIVE:
Explain prompt engineering to beginners with a technical background.

CONSTRAINTS:

Keep the explanation under 200 words

Use clear bullet points

Avoid marketing language or hype

Focus on practical principles, not history

OUTPUT FORMAT:

Short introduction

4â€“6 bullet points

One concise concluding sentence

SELF-CHECK:
Before finalizing, verify that the explanation is clear,
concise, and free of vague statements.

### Why This Works
- The role anchors expertise
- Constraints reduce ambiguity
- The output format controls structure
- The self-check improves reliability
